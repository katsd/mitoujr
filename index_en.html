About

What is Mitou Jr?

Mitou Program for U-17 talented creators and programmers in Japan.

If you don't know what is Mitou Program in Japan, please refer this page

Mentoring

Tremendous learning opportunity from PMs and other Mitou graduates who are at the forefront of technology.

Financial support

Each group will be eligible to receive up to 500K yen as development fund.

Location of development

As necessary, location of developemt will be provided.

Recommendation for Mitou Program

Creators certified as Mitou Junior Super Creator will be eligible to be recommended to next year's Mitou Program.

Anki Cookie

Ayaka Nakasuji (Ukai PM)

Summary

Anki-cookie is to help motivate students through personalized cookies with prints based on their result from web quizzes.

Message from Ukai PM

Based on her personal experience, she developed a system named Anki-cookie which fosters motivation to learn kanjis among students who live in foreign countries by engraving the remembered kanjis on cookies as a reward. The fact that she made at least working prototype in very limited time should really be praised. I’d like her to continuously challenge opening up the possibility of the great idea.

Summary

Message from Yasukawa PM

Despite being a High School student, Ohno and Suzuki already acquired the skill enough to live off, so through this project they were able to focus on learning the development of product making. During the short term, they experienced the following phase of product: releasing, learning from user testing, and publishing their product ( https://vamboo.net/ ) as an opensource, which should be one of the most common experiences in the near future. I hope they will make use of Mitou community in order to push the boundaries of the community and shape the unknown possibility that this community has.

Raku Raku(easy)Yomi Yomi(reading)

Ray Ohtsuka (Suzuki PM)

Summary

This is an application which enables easy reading of articles for people who suffer from weak eyesight. The functionality is full of ingenious combination; displaying the characters, highlighting the area which is already being read, and tracking line of sight.

Project Managers (for 2017)

Koichi Sasada

Cookpad Inc.
Biography

He developed a virtual machine for Ruby named YARV, and it was adopted as a part of Ruby 1.9 in 2007. He's been contributing to Ruby as a committer mainly for speed-up related projects such as performance improvement of virtual machines, and garbage collection. He received Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Tokyo. He was also certified as Mitou Super Creator in 2004.

Hirokazu Nishio

Cybozu Lab
Biography

He was certified as Mitou Super Creator in 2002. He also received Ph.D. in Science in 2006, and Master of Management of Technology in 2014. From 2007, he's been working for Cybozu Lab, and working on research projects related to team collaboration software. He published several books in Japanese such as 「コーディングを支える技術」「word2vecによる自然言語処理」. He's also a board member of Mitou Foundation.

Yohei Yasukawa

YassLab, CoderDojo Japan
Biography

CEO of YassLab, Representative of CoderDojo Japan. He received his master's degree in Computer Science from Waseda University, and was certified as Mitou Super Creator. After studyin abroad in the United States, he founded YassLab company that focuses on agile development, education, and translation technology. He likes Ruby/Rails and publishes Japanese Rails Tutorial and Rails Guides, which are now two of most famous resources in Japanese Ruby community. Using those resources he lectures Web Development at Japanese universities such as AiiT and University of Tsukuba.

Taizan Yonetsuji

Preferred Networks
Biography

He belonged to RoboCon club in the University of Tokyo, and was responsible for mechanics. He was also a creator of Mitou Project in 2012. After he received master's degree from the University of Tokyo, he worked for CNC software research institution in Fanuc corporation, and then joined Preferred Networks in 2016. He has been working on Amazon Picking Challenge and other robotics projects. He's best known for PaintsChainer which is automatic painting service using Chainer deep-learning framework developed by Preferred Networks.

Ryo Suzuki

Waseda Univ.
Biography

He was certified as Mitou Super Creator in 2013 by his achievement on developing C++ library for game and media-art project named Siv3D. He's also been responsible for C++ programming classes for Information Science school for middle and high school students run by Waseda University.

Daiki Teramoto

HackforPlay
Biography

He's been developing HackforPlay to make more students feel coding fun. He was certified as Mitou Super Creator in 2015. He's also a founder of CoderDojo Kanazawa.

Yoshifumi Seki

Gunosy Inc.
Biography

He received Ph.D. in Engineering from the University of Tokyo. He was also a Mitou Creator in 2011. When he was a postgraduate student, he co-developed Gunosy, and eventually co-founded the company. He's been responsible for news delivery logic in the company, and he had deep expertise on web-mining focusing on recommendation systems, the application of machine learning, and natural language processing. Additionally, he's a visiting researcher at the University of Tokyo from April of 2017.

Yu Ukai

King's College London
Biography

He was certified as a Mitou Super Creator in 2011 as a result of his achievement in the development of an underwater robot for swimming education. After he received master's degree in Arts and Science from the University of Tokyo, he had been working for Microsoft as a Program Manager in Office and Minecraft Education team. He designed many educational products such as Office Lens, Minecraft: Education Edition and Minecraft Hour of Code. Now, he left the company and he is a postgraduate student at King's College London for Computer Science Education.

Supervisor

Ikuo Takeuchi

Honorary Professor at the University of Tokyo, Director of Mitou Project, Director of Mitou Foundation
Biography

He was born in Toyama in 1946. After he joined NTT Research Laboratory in 1971, he worked on Lisp and the development of its processing system. After he left the company, he was a professor at the University of Electro-Communications, the University of Tokyo and Waseda University.
